Music and Human Connection

Bridging Cultural Divides Through the Universal Language of Music - By Aaron Zhou

Research Focus

My project explores how music functions as a transformative force that forges social bonds across diverse cultures. In an increasingly fragmented world, music serves not only as entertainment but also as a powerful tool to foster empathy and build community.

Research Question

How do shared musical experiences create collective senses of belonging and foster meaningful connections in a digitally isolated society? This inquiry examines the interplay between traditional musical practices and modern digital platforms.

Case Studies & Evidence

Through a blend of archival research and contemporary case studies, the project analyzes communal music practices in traditional societies alongside the evolution of digital musical engagement. By comparing these diverse contexts, I aim to reveal the universal aspects of music that transcend cultural boundaries.

Implications

The findings of this project offer valuable insights for scholars, educators, and community organizers. By leveraging music's transformative impact, communities can find innovative ways to reconnect in an era of digital isolation.
