Music and Human Connection

Bridging Cultural Divides Through the Universal Language of Music – By Aaron Zhou

Research Focus

My project explores how music functions as a transformative force that forges social bonds across diverse cultures. In an increasingly fragmented world, music serves not only as entertainment but also as a powerful tool to foster empathy and build community.

Goal: To demonstrate how shared musical experiences—both traditional and digital—can rebuild social bonds and promote cross-cultural understanding.

Research Question

How do shared musical experiences create collective senses of belonging and foster meaningful connections in a digitally isolated society? This inquiry examines the interplay between traditional musical practices and modern digital platforms.

Case Studies & Evidence

Through a blend of archival research and contemporary case studies, the project analyzes communal music practices in traditional societies alongside the evolution of digital musical engagement. By comparing these diverse contexts, I aim to reveal the universal aspects of music that transcend cultural boundaries. Key sources include Tom A. Garner’s How the World Listens, Abraham Santos Fernández’s narrative How Music Taught Me to Listen, and the 1949 Lantern article School of Music Gets Grand Piano as Gift.

Implications

The findings of this project offer valuable insights for scholars, educators, and community organizers. By leveraging music’s transformative impact—through traditional gatherings and digital platforms like Spotify and YouTube—communities can find innovative ways to reconnect in an era of digital isolation.

Join the conversation: How can we harness music to rebuild our social bonds?
